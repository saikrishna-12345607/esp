Key Features and Mechanics:
Gameplay: The snake moves automatically in one of four directions (up, down, left, right), controlled by the player.
Objective: Eat randomly appearing food (dots or items) to increase in length and maximize the score.
Obstacles: The snake must avoid hitting the boundary walls and its own growing body.
Origin: The concept originated from the 1976 arcade game Blockade, and gained immense popularity as a pre-loaded game on Nokia phones in the late 1990s.
Variations: Modern versions often include different speeds, wall-less modes, or maze obstacles, but the core mechanic remains the same.
